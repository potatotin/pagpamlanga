Delicacies

Culinary scene is a tapestry woven from tradition, local flavors, and a rich cultural heritage. Nestled amidst rolling hills and scenic landscapes, M'lang proudly showcases its unique delicacies, each dish a testament to its culinary legacy.

Alupi

It's made of grated cassava, coconut milk,meat, sugar, and other flavorings. Then it's steamed while wrapped in banana leaves.

Apan-apan

It is an Ilonggo dish that is considered as a version of Adobong kangkong often mixed with pork and sometimes with ginamos.

Aripahol

In Ilonggo, it's a native delicacy made of kamoteng kahoy or balinghoy. It is a steamed cassava (grated) with sugar or mascuvado on top.

Balbacua

Is a Filipino beef stew made of beef, collagen rich beef components (oxtail, skin, and joints), and a variety of spices that is simmered for several hours until very tender.

Batchoy

Pork offal, crumbled pork cracklings, chicken stock, beef loin, and round noodles are used in this noodle soup.

Baye-baye

It is a traditional Ilonggo dessert made with a combination of grated young coconut and either corn patties or young rice that's pounded into flakes (pinipig).

Bitso-bitso

It is an Ilonggo kakanin made of glutinous rice flour, coated with caramelized sugar.

Bulalo

It's a light-colored soup cooked by simmering beef shanks and bone marrow in a clear broth until the collagen and fat have dissolved. Leafy vegetables (such as pechay or cabbage), corn on the cob, scallions, onions, garlic, ginger, and fish sauce are common ingredients.

Dinakdakan

Dinakdakan is a traditional delicacy prepared with grilled pig face and liver that is made richer and creamier by adding pork brains or mayonnaise instead. Perfect for lunch or dinner as a main course with steamed rice, or as "pulutan" (booze food).

Ibus

Suman sa Ibos, also known as Ibus, is a Filipino rice cake prepared from glutinous rice cooked in coconut milk and often wrapped in coconut leaves.

Inday-inday

This is a simple delicacy made of sticky rice flour (malagkit) cooked in boiling water, similar to palitaw, and topped with bucayo, gelatinous coconut meat boiled in water, muscovado sugar, and coconut milk.

Inubarang Manok

The term "inubaran" refers to a dish made with ubad. This dish is a soup made with chicken (best if native chicken) and ubad (banana pith).

KBL

Kadyos, Baboy, and Langka, the famous dish's major ingredients. It's made with pigeon peas (kadyos), pork (baboy), and unripe jackfruit (langka) and served with a sour broth flavored with Batwan.

Fried Kokak

It's an exotic cuisine in which palakang bukid is the main ingredient and is deep fried.

Laswa/Law-oy

Laswa is a boiled vegetable soup made from a diverse but harmonious mix of ingredients.

Palitaw

Palitaw is made with a dough made from glutinous rice flour and water, shaped into balls, boiled until soft and chewy, then rolled in coconut and sugar.

Sinamak

It is an Ilonggo version of spicy vinegar. Several spices were blended and steeped in vinegar for several days to weeks to give this condiment its appeal and taste.

Suman Latik

Is an ilonggo native delicacy made primarily of plain suman and sweetened coconut strips or bukayo

Sinigang

Is a soup or stew with a sour and savory flavor. It's most commonly associated with tamarind, but it can also be made with other sour fruits and leaves.

Adobong Takway

Adobo nga takway is almost identical to apan-apan (water spinach). It's made with shrimp paste or soy sauce and a long and slender stolon of taro.

Valenciana

It's a Filipino version Paella. Glutinous or ordinary rice, chicken, chorizo, butter, onion, red bell pepper, tomatoes, peas, and salt and pepper to taste make up this dish.
